Employee Retention Credit (ERC)

Get up to $26,000 per employee from the IRS. It's a cash refund, not a loan.

Businesses have claimed an average of $130,000 each

The Employee Retention Credit (ERC), also known as the Employee Retention Tax Credit (ERTC), is a federal program created to help small and medium-sized businesses recover from the financial impact of COVID-19. It's separate from PPP and is a refundable tax credit paid directly by the IRS — it never has to be repaid.

Who Can Qualify?

Full or Partial Suspension

A federal, state or local shutdown order affected your ability to operate in 2020 or 2021.

Revenue Reduction

Gross revenue during 2020-2021 declined quarter-over-quarter versus 2019.

Recovery Startup Business

Started after Feb 15, 2020 with annual gross revenue under $1,000,000.

FAQs

What is my maximum possible credit?

Up to $26,000 per employee, though most businesses qualify for less than the maximum — we help you get the highest refund you're eligible for.

My revenue didn't drop during COVID — am I still eligible?

Possibly. You may still qualify if you were affected by a partial or full government shutdown, among other criteria.

If I received a PPP loan, can I still qualify?

Yes, in many cases. PPP forgiveness may reduce your ERC in certain quarters, and our CPAs can help you maximize it.

When will I receive my refund?

It can take several months to over a year through the standard IRS process; our advance payment option can speed this up significantly.
