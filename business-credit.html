Business Credit 101

Unlock easier financing, better supplier terms and stronger negotiating power by building strong business credit.

Building your own business credit — separate from your personal credit — can open the door to small business loans, better financing terms, and stronger relationships with suppliers and lenders. It's also worth remembering that many lenders check personal credit too, especially for newer businesses, so keeping both in good shape matters.

How Business Credit Gets Reported

Business credit is reported to major bureaus such as Equifax Small Business and Experian Business — but the bureau lenders rely on most is Dun & Bradstreet. Not every company or bank reports to every bureau, which is why it can feel inconsistent from the outside.

To start building business credit, you'll need to operate under a proper business name with an EIN, business address, phone number, and any licenses or permits your industry requires. If you need help setting this up correctly, our advisors can guide you through business formation.

Vendors That Report Payment History

  • Newegg Business Net-30 — office electronics
  • Quill Credit Net-30 — office supplies and furniture

A Few Things To Keep In Mind

  • You may not always know when your business credit is being reviewed — staying informed matters
  • Good business credit doesn't exempt you from personal credit checks on many loan types
  • You can dispute inaccurate items on your personal credit directly with the bureaus before paying for credit repair services
